## 2. Key per-platform observations

- **Rosebud** is the closest existing analog to the planned product: prompt → hosted link → community feed → remix templates → MP/leaderboards via a dedicated backend template. Its weaknesses per third-party reviews ([Summer Engine review](https://www.summerengine.com/blog/is-rosebud-ai-worth-it), [Soonlab](https://www.soonlab.ai/blog/rosebud-ai-review/)): features break unexpectedly, prompt phrasing lottery ("add an enemy" is nondeterministic), no export/ownership (hosted page only), browser-3D performance ceiling, and only ~80% AI-completion with weak manual tools for the last 20%.
- **Astrocade** proves the money thesis ($56M, Sequoia) and mobile+remix as the loop, but even friendly reviews note "AI generation can feel unpredictable, with mixed quality depending on prompts" and rivals dismiss the output as shallow mini-games.
- **Upit's** real asset is FRVR's portal distribution (funnels its existing player traffic to creator games) — nobody else solves the *players* side. Multiplayer/keyboard/3D still on the roadmap, i.e., desktop-quality games are out of scope.
- **Roblox Build** is the incumbent threat: mobile prompt-to-game inside the app with 3D foundation models, but locked inside Roblox's walled garden, aesthetic, and age dynamics; its retention-ranked discovery is the design pattern to copy.
- **Vibe-coding platforms**: games get made as one-offs and die at the deploy link — no leaderboards, no discovery, no matchmaking. The exception proves the gap: **fly.pieter.com** — Cursor/Claude-built 3-hour Three.js MMO prototype, ~100M X impressions, [$1M ARR in 17 days](https://levels.io/fly-pieter-com-vibecoded-flight-simulator) via sponsor billboards, and levelsio's Vibe Jam. [404 Media's take](https://www.404media.co/this-game-created-by-ai-vibe-coding-makes-50-000-a-month-yours-probably-wont/): the revenue came from a 10-year audience, not the tooling — i.e., distribution, not generation, was the scarce ingredient.

## 3. Where AI-generated games fail (recurring modes)

1. **Prompt nondeterminism / regression**: edits break working mechanics; "not all issues have clear solutions" (Rosebud community). Multi-turn drift makes iteration a gamble.
2. **Samey output**: freeform generation converges on the same ~15 archetypes (snake, flappy, tower defense, endless runner); Websim/Astrocade feeds are dominated by near-duplicates.
3. **The last 20%**: AI reaches "playable demo," but tuning (game feel, difficulty curves, juice) has no affordance in chat UIs — exactly what Poki/CrazyGames data says decides retention.
4. **Mobile/controls**: generated games default to keyboard; touch controls, aspect ratios, and portrait mode are afterthoughts (CrazyGames disables Safari-broken games outright).
5. **No ownership/portability**: hosted-page lock-in (Rosebud) blocks graduation to Steam/stores, capping creator ambition.
6. **Performance ceiling**: browser 3D from generated code is heavy; violates the <8-20MB, sub-3-second bars portals enforce.
7. **Slop flood + stigma**: itch.io's AI flood forced disclosure/filter policies; Steam data shows [up to 53% worse sales](https://www.techpowerup.com/350230/games-made-with-gen-ai-suffer-up-to-53-worse-sales-on-steam) for gen-AI games; [85% of players negative](https://quanticfoundry.com/2025/12/18/gen-ai/) on gen-AI (Quantic Foundry). Discovery without retention-ranking drowns in slop.
8. **What works**: constrained generation. The best results come from fixed engines (Summer/Godot, Roblox), curated primitives/templates (Rosebud's MP template, Jabali genre templates, Upit's 22-agent pipeline with error-checking), and "one prompt per mechanic" rather than one-shot whole-game generation. Research literature agrees: constraint solvers + curated primitive pools beat unconstrained generation.

## 4. The distribution bar: Poki & CrazyGames

- **Poki SDK requirements** ([sdk.poki.com](https://sdk.poki.com/new-requirements)): initial download **<8MB**, playable in seconds, no login, no splash screens, direct-to-gameplay, skippable cutscenes, visual (not text) tutorials, full mobile+desktop support at 16:9, gameplayStart/Stop + commercialBreak event hygiene, works in incognito and with adblock. Their guidance: successful games are "playable in under 3 seconds," show the core fun immediately; long intros/menus/tutorials kill conversion.
- **CrazyGames** ([docs](https://docs.crazygames.com/requirements/quality/)): ≤50MB (≤20MB for mobile homepage), ≤1500 files, Chrome/Edge/Safari support, onboarding-in-gameplay, consistent art style, regional keyboard layouts, PEGI-12.
- **Implication**: this is the objective quality bar for generated games. An AI pipeline that *automatically enforces* portal-grade constraints (size, instant-load, touch controls, onboarding-in-gameplay) would differentiate output quality more than a better model would.

## 5. Remix culture as the creation-side growth loop

- **Websim**: every creation is a URL; every edit of someone else's creation is an attributed fork. Zero-friction lineage turned consumers into creators and drove its 2024 virality (YouTube creators making "AI internet with games" content).
- **Mario Maker**: Nintendo shipped a mapmaker, not levels — the loop "see cool level → buy game → make level → others see it" ran with minimal Nintendo input; streamer content (kaizo, Trials of Death) was the amplifier.
- **PICO-8**: fixed console constraints (128×128, token limits) + carts that ship with source = every game is a learnable, remixable artifact; constraints made output *coherent and recognizable*, not samey.
- **Astrocade/Upit/Rosebud** all now treat remix + attribution as core. Pattern: remix converts the 99% who won't start from a blank prompt; attribution + visible lineage is the status economy that motivates originals.

## 6. Strategic openings for a new entrant

1. **Own the player side, not just creation.** Everyone funds creation; only FRVR/Roblox have players. Build the Poki-grade portal (instant load, retention-ranked feed) where AI games *compete for real players* — Roblox's "if no one plays it, no one can find it" retention-ranking as anti-slop discovery.
2. **Constrained engine, not freeform code.** A fixed, tiny, portal-compliant runtime (curated primitives: controller, physics, spawner, scoring, juice presets) — PICO-8-style constraints make generated games faster, less broken, and stylistically coherent. *(Note: founder chose pure-freeform + agentic verification instead; this opening is addressed via CONVENTIONS.md + judges rather than an SDK.)*
3. **Leaderboards/multiplayer as platform services, not generated code.** Rosebud makes AI write networking; instead ship managed leaderboards, ghosts, matchmaking, invites as one-line primitives — the single hardest thing for LLMs to generate reliably.
4. **Mobile/touch-first generation.** Nearly all competitors generate keyboard games; portals say mobile is where traffic is. Enforce touch controls + portrait support at generation time.
5. **Websim-grade remix lineage** ("everything is a fork," visible family trees, attribution) combined with game-specific hooks: "beat this score, then remix the game."
6. **Auto-QA agent as moat**: play-test the generated game (headless), verify win/lose states, controls, and frame rate before publish — directly attacks failure mode #1; Upit's error-checking agents are the only comparable effort.
7. **The "last 20%" tuning UI**: sliders for difficulty/speed/juice extracted from generated code, so non-coders can do game-feel iteration without prompt roulette.
8. **Creator payouts gated on plays** (Astrocade's 100K-plays model) + sponsor-slot monetization (fly.pieter.com's proven $70-90K MRR pattern) rather than tip jars.
9. **Speed-to-viral-moment**: shareable challenge links (score-attached URLs) turn every play session into distribution; nobody currently fuses the Wordle-share mechanic with generated games.
10. **Avoid the stigma trap**: brand around "games with your friends in 60 seconds," not "AI-generated games" — player sentiment on gen-AI is 85% negative; the product is the social loop, AI is the implementation detail.
